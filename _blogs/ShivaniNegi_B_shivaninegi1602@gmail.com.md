One day, I was half asleep when my mother said, "You got a message on your phone." I casually picked it up... and in that moment, my sleep disappeared completely.

It was a message about the Vinternship by the Vicharanashala (Lab for Education Design) at Indian Institute of Technology, Ropar .

I don't know how to fully describe that feeling - but I suddenly felt extremely excited. Maybe because at that time I was already doing an NPTEL course and seeing this opportunity felt like something bigger - like a chance to actually experience something bigger - like a chance to actually experience learning, not just watch it.

I had only one expectation:

> *to learn as much as I can and apply it in my life.*

---

### The Beginning: Excitement with a Little Fear


I always had an interest in coding. I had learned a bit of C++ and Python before, but web development was completely new to me.

JavaScript felt different... but not entirely unfamiliar.

I was curious about everything -

* What is MERN ?
* How does an internship actually work?
* What will I learn?

But along with excitement, there was a fear:

> "Will I be able to manage college and this internship together?"

---

### ViBe: A Completely New Way of Learning


The ViBe platform was a totally new experience for me. 

On the first day, I started Nandan Prabhudesai Sir's JavaScript introduction class. There was this "penalty score", and I initially thought that a higher score meant better performance.

The next day, I sat alone in complete silence, expecting my score to increase... But it stayed near zero.

That's when I realized - lower score was actually better.

Sometimes it showed "multiple faces detected" when I was sitting alone. Sometimes "no face detected" when I was right in front of the screen.

It was funny at times, frustrating at others.

But one thing was clear - this system was making me more disciplined.

---

### Small Videos, Big Lessons


After every small video, there was a quiz.

And if I missed even one line... that exact line would appear as a question.

At first, it felt annoying.

But slowly I understood - this was building my focus.

Over time, I started watching every video with full concentration.

And honestly, Nandan Sir's teaching style just wow.

---

### The Moment I Felt Lost


After completing my ViBe, when I tried to start case studies...

I didn't understand anything.

"Where do I start? What am I supposed to do? How do I even begin?"

That was the moment I felt completely stuck.

Then I saw a message on Discord from JEFFY J :

"Have you felt stucked how to start with case studies?"

I instantly replied with "Yes".

That one message changed everything.

A breakout session was arranged, and for the first time, I interacted with others in the internship - Varada Surve , Rishabh Ranjan , Abhishek Sinha and JEFFY J .

And I realized something important:

>  "The problems I was facing... everyone else was facing them too."

That day, I understood - I was not alone.

---

### Beyond Coding: What I Really Learned


This internship was not just about coding.

It taught me:

* How to actually learn
* How to think deeply about a problem
* What consistency truly means

I remember in inaugural session, Sudarshan Iyengar Sir said:

> "I don't know what hard work or smart work is... but consistency is important."
At that time, I just heard it.

Now, I understand it.

---

### Consistency Changed Everything


Earlier, I used to focus on solving more questions.

Now, I focus on understanding one question deeply.

It takes more time, but gives real clarity.

There is hardly a day now when I :

Don't open Discord
Don't code
Don't try to learn something

And every time I solve a case study, I feel:

> "Yes... I can do this."

---

### The People Who Made It Special


Breakout rooms are honestly unforgettable.

We sit for hours solving problems, discussing doubts, and helping each other.

Standups and Live Sessions with Rohit Sharma Sir, Jinal Gupta Mam, Nitin Sankar Sir, Riya Mehta Mam...

These are not just sessions - *they are experiences.*

---

### The Biggest Change


If I had to describe this journey in one word: 

**"Transforming"**

It completely changed how I think about learning.

If I could say something to my past self, it would be:

> "Don't overthink. Just start."

---

### Not Just an Internship


I am most proud of being a part of this journey.

This internship doesn't just teach skills - It teaches you how to learn, how to manage, and how to grow.

If someone asks me whether they should join...

I would simply say:

> "Definitely join - but don't just complete it... experience it."

---

I am truly grateful to be part of this journey and to learn alongside such amazing people.
